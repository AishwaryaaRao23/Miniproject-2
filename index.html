Let's Explore LADAKH!!
Pangong Tso

Pangong Tso or Pangong lake is one of the most famous Ladakh lakes, the Pangong Lake is 150 KM long and extends from India to China. The crystal blue waters of the lake get more charmed up with the panoramic views of mountains standing guard behind it.

Shanti Stupa

Shanti Stupa is a Buddhist white-domed Stupa (chorten) on a hilltop in Chanspa, Leh district, Ladakh, in north India.

Puga Valley

Puga Valley is situated in the Changthang Valley in the south-eastern part of Ladakh, about 22 km away from the Salt Lake Valley. It is a region of great significance known for its natural beauty and geothermal activities.

Mirpal Tso

Mirpal lake: If looking for a place filled with solitude and serenity, then the shores of Mirpal Tso are made for you. Exhibiting vast scenery of clear waters, the lake is isolated from the hustle and bustle of the city and stays very calm.

Kargil

Kargil or Kargyil is a city in Indian-administered Ladakh in the Kashmir region. It is the joint capital of Ladakh, an Indian-administered union territory. It is also the headquarters of the Kargil district. It is the second-largest city in Ladakh after Leh.

Khardung La

Khardung La or Khardung Pass is a mountain pass in the Leh district of the Indian union territory of Ladakh. The pass is on the Ladakh Range, north of Leh, and connects the Indus river valley and the Shyok river valley.

The Snow Land

Ladakh, large area of the northern and eastern Kashmir region, northwestern Indian subcontinent. Administratively, Ladakh is divided between Pakistan (northwest), as part of Gilgit-Baltistan, and India (southeast), as part of Ladakh union territory. Ladakh covers about 45,000 square miles (117,000 square km) and contains the Ladakh Range, which is a southeastern extension of the Karakoram Range, and the upper Indus River valley.
